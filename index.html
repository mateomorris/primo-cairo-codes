Cairo Codes

Cairo Codes is an early-stage initiative in partnership with St. Andrew's Refugee Services (StARS) that aims to train refugees and migrants living in Cairo, Egypt, how to become Full-Stack Developers. As of fall of 2019, it has maintained a class of 7 hard-working individuals from a variety of countries and backgrounds.

Why Cairo Codes

Cairo, Egypt, is home to a large community of refugee and migrant groups. The majority of refugees and migrants have fled their countries, arriving in Cairo with little to no resources. With limited opportunities for resettlement, most refugees end up residing in Cairo for long periods without sufficient access to the labor market. 

As a result, the majority of refugees resort to working in the informal economy to survive where they face dangerous work conditions, extremely low-wages, and exploitation. The Cairo Codes team views the Cairo Codes initiative as one means to support refugees and migrants in accessing safe and sustainable employment in the software industry.

Program Background

In the Summer of 2018, with support from StARS, the Cairo Codes team began to create a programming curriculum that would be easily understandable and applicable for students who have little to no experience in programming. The Team designed the curriculum based on current technology trends, marketplace demand, and insight from tech firms and businesses. 

By Spring 2019, the team opened a call for refugees and migrants working at StARS to apply for the Cairo Codes program. After thoroughly reviewing 60+ applications, the Team admitted 20 students (20 being the maximum limit for how many students the team could teach and support). Since then, the students have been learning the basics of programming, CSS, HTML, and JavaScript. The next phase of the course will consist of learning popular Javascript frameworks such as React and Svelte and working on group projects to build web applications for local and international businesses and NGOs.

Cairo Codes aims to equip migrants and refugees to obtain meaningful and sustainable employment through becoming marketable Full-Stack Developers. This will be achieved through delivering an intense 9-month coding boot camp, which provides students with up-to-date, hands-on instruction on Full-Stack Javascript and developer tools. The course is divided into the following phases:

  • Building blocks of programming: JavaScript, HTML, CSS

  • Build tools, Git, Publishing

  • Individual Projects and Portfolio Building

  • Professional Development

  • Mentorship and Next Steps Session

Additionally, Cairo Codes seeks to provide job opportunities for students to start working full-time following the completion of the course.

Ways to Support

  • Personal Laptops for students (right now students are using their work laptops)

  • Funding for wifi for students who do not have access to wifi at home

  • Partnerships with employers willing to offer internships and full-time positions to course graduates
